Best for individuals who are: -

  • Seeking to review Montessori pedagogy & practice
  • Working in Montessori adolescent learning environments and seeking greater knowledge about the Montessori approach and who are
  • In a position to only audit the diploma course without doing assignments or practicum.

Best for individuals who are: -

  • Holding a current nationally recognised vocational credential (in education or other vocational field) or working towards a credential through an accredited tertiary institution
  • Seeking to work in Montessori adolescent learning environments as either generalist or specialist teachers, or expert facilitators of activities
  • Unable to attend in-person sessions and hold a current nationally recognised vocational credential and want to work in a Montessori learning environment for adolescents.

Best for individuals who are: -

  • Holding a current nationally recognised credential (in education or other vocational field) or working towards such a credential through an accredited tertiary institution
  • Seeking to establish a Montessori learning environment for adolescents
  • Seeking to work in Montessori adolescent learning environments as either generalist or specialist teachers, or expert facilitators of activities
  • Seeking an internationally recognised Montessori credential.

This course develops an understanding of Montessori philosophy and practice, for new teachers and hired experts working in established adolescent environments and for those who are looking for a refresher course in adolescent education.

This course develops a deeper understanding of Montessori philosophy and practice and supports the skill development of adults new to Montessori working in adolescent environments.

This course develops a deeper understanding of Montessori philosophy and practice and provides intensive onsite training to support the skill development of teachers and other expert adults new to Montessori working in adolescent environments or who are in the process of establishing a Montessori learning environment for adolescents.

1000hrs Self-reflective Practicum

The practicum includes the following, self-monitored:-

  • teaching practise
  • observation activities
  • interaction between adult learners and learning material
  • interaction between adult learners and adolescents
  • integration of knowledge with practise
  • preparation and care of the environment
  • communications
  • demonstration of skills

Adult learners will complete and submit a reflection activity based upon their practicum experiences to include in their professional Reflective Journal.
